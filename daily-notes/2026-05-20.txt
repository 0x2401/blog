Ways to write when you don't have ideas:
- Read other people's writing. Summarize it. Extract the ideas and rewrite it in your own words. Rewrite it several different ways - like normal, like Tolkien, like a Founding Father. Go through each claim and ask yourself if it's true or not, even obvious ones.
- Read papers and summarize their key points. Trace their intellectual lineage.
- Brain dump your thoughts
- Write words that sound nice together. Look through the dictionary and try to find cute words that sound nice or with cool definitions. Use them in a sentence.
- Don't write new stuff at all. Just collect interesting things with commentary.
- Post a question. Don't put an answer - just a question.
- Share reading notes (this means you have to read).
- Learn something new every day and then write about it.
- Commit blog posts. Write out short summaries of a commit - the problem that existed, how it solved it, and why it was solved this particular way.
- Worldbuild. Write out interesting ideas for fictional characters, universes, companies, mythologies, and religions.
- Critique other writing. Notetake on the structure of other writing - how does it flow? Why was it written the way it is? Why does that structure make it sound good? Analyze.
- Try writing in longhand.
- A topic is not a story.
